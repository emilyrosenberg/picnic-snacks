All about classic picnic snacks

Traditional and delicious, these are the crowd-pleasers of every picnic. From a savory dip with seven layers of flavor, to sandwiches of all kinds, to chocolate chip cookies, find all the classic picnic treats right here.

Seven layer dip

Savory and fun, this dip brings the party.

Ingredients

  • One 16-oz can refried beans
  • 3 large ripe Hass avocados, mashed with 3 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 cups sour cream mixed with one packet taco seasoning
  • 3 tablespoons chopped drained canned green chiles
  • Two 5 3/4-oz cans sliced black olives, drained
  • 2 cups sharp grated cheddar
  • Chopped cilantro or scallions
  • Tortilla or pita chips to serve

Instructions

  1. In a 9 x 13 inch baking dish, spread a layer of each ingredient in the order listed.
  2. Refrigerate until needed, and wrap tightly to transport.
  3. Serve with tortilla or pita chips.

Sandwiches for everyone

Pick one of these variations or make more to share.

Ingredients

  • Sliced meats with lettuce, tomato, mustard and/or mayonnaise
  • Peanut butter and jelly
  • Sliced tomato with fresh mozzerella
  • White, wheat or other sliced bread

Instructions

  1. For meat or cheese sandwiches, spread one slice of bread with mayonnaise and/or mustard, layer the other ingredients, and top with the second slice.
  2. For peanut butter and jelly sandwiches, spread one ingredient on each slice and put them together.
  3. Wrap the sandwiches and label them so everyone can easily find their favorite!

Chocolate chip cookies

A favorite for everyone, makes 36 cookies.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup or one stick unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 F. Grease or line 2 cookie sheets.
  2. Whisk together the flour and baking soda.
  3. Beat the butter and 2 types of sugar in a large bowl until blended.
  4. Add the egg, salt, and vanilla, and beat until well combined.
  5. Stir in the flour mixture, and stir until smooth.
  6. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  7. Drop the dough by heaping teaspoonfuls about two inches apart onto the cookie sheets.
  8. Bake until the cookies are just slightly colored on top and the edges are brown, 8 to 10 minutes.
  9. Let stand briefly, then move to a rack to cool.
