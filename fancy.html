All about fancy picnic snacks

Picnic, but make it elegant! Just bring along a bottle of wine or even champagne, spread a lovely blanket with the finest snacks, and enjoy yourself in style.

Charcuterie board

What fancy picnic would be complete without one?

Ingredients

  • Assorted cheeses, for instance brie, chevre, manchego, grana padano, and roquefort
  • Assorted cured meats, for instance salami, prosciutto, and a terrine or pâté
  • Olives
  • Fruit spread like membrillo or fig jam
  • Grapes, strawberries, or other fresh fruit
  • Salted or spiced nuts
  • Baguettes and crackers to serve

Instructions

  1. Bring a large cutting board or platter with you, along with cheese knives and a few smaller dishes and spoons, and all your ingredients.
  2. Once you get to your picnic spot, set it up! Place your cheeses on the board, and nestle folded slices of cured meat and fresh fruit between them along with bowls of fig jam, olives, and salted nuts.
  3. Dig in! But in a fancy way.

Tomato goat cheese quiche

Elegant, fresh, and practical.

Ingredients

  • One prepared pie crust
  • Beaten egg yolk
  • 1 pound tomatoes
  • 6 oz soft fresh goat cheese
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons chopped thyme or 3 tablespoons chopped basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Brush the inside of the pie crust with beaten egg yolk and refrigerate.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400 F.
  3. Seed, core, and quarter the tomatoes.
  4. Crumble the goat cheese and blend with the cream and milk until smooth.
  5. Add the eggs, herbs, salt, and pepper to the cheese mixture, and whisk until smooth.
  6. Arrange the tomato quarters in the pie crust and pour the cheese mixture over them.
  7. Bake until the top is golden brown, about 45 minutes.
  8. Cool for at least 10 minutes, or refrigerate and then return to room temperature before serving.

Butterscotch brownies

Delightfully luxurious yet familiar.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2/3 cup light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 large egg plus 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon light corn syrup
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla
  • 1 cup chopped, toasted pecans

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 F. Grease an 8-inch-square baking pan lined with foil.
  2. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a large heavy saucepan, melt the butter and then boil until light golden brown, about 4 minutes.
  4. Remove from the heat, add both types of sugar, and stir until well blended.
  5. Let cool until barely warm.
  6. Stir in the egg and egg yolk, corn syrup, and vanilla until well combined.
  7. Stir in the flour mixture and pecans.
  8. Scrape into the pan and bake until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, 25 to 30 minutes.
  9. Cool completely in the pan on a rack, and cut into squares to serve.
